# RetailEdge Inc. — Financial Performance Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyses the financial performance of RetailEdge Inc., 
a multi-state retail company operating across 6 states in the US 
between 2020 and 2025. The analysis investigates a revenue decline 
that began after the 2022 peak and identifies which sub-categories, 
states and payment modes are driving or destroying value.

---

## ❓ Business Problem
> "RetailEdge Inc. has experienced strong growth followed by an 
> unexpected decline. Revenue peaked at $1.46M in 2022 and has 
> fallen for two consecutive years. Profit is declining faster 
> than revenue. We operate across 6 states, 3 categories and 
> 12 sub-categories serving 802 customers but we have no clarity 
> on which markets and products are driving value and which are 
> destroying it."

---

## 🔍 Insight Questions
1. What caused the revenue decline after the 2022 peak?
2. Which sub-categories generate the highest profit margin?
3. Which states are underperforming relative to their cost burden?
4. Which payment mode is the least efficient?
5. Is the business growing or declining year-on-year?

## 💡 Key Findings
- Revenue peaked at **$1.46M in 2022** and declined **17.6%** 
  over two years
- **Bookcases (28.6%)**, Paper (28.5%) and Chairs (28.4%) are 
  the most profitable sub-categories but the most underinvested
- **Phones (22.6%)** consume 8.1% of revenue but return only 
  7.1% of profit — a negative imbalance
- **COD payment mode** has the lowest margin at 22.4% — costing 
  an estimated $37,820 in annual profit
- **Florida** delivers the highest profit margin (28.3%) but 
  receives less sales attention than New York


---

## ✅ Recommendations
1. Prioritise Bookcases, Paper and Chairs in procurement and 
   marketing — highest margin, most underinvested
2. Review Phones and Pens product lines — revenue share 
   exceeds profit share consistently
3. Discourage COD through digital payment incentives to recover 
   ~$37K in annual profit
4. Expand focus on Florida — highest margin state at 28.3%
5. Investigate Electronics collapse — 40.8% revenue drop in 2024
